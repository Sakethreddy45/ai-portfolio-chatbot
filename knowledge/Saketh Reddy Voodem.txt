PROJECTS

Project: AI Portfolio Chatbot

Built a chatbot that works as an interactive portfolio where visitors can ask anything about Saketh's background and get real answers. Under the hood it runs a RAG pipeline. Resume and project docs get chunked and embedded using OpenAI's text-embedding-3-small model then stored in ChromaDB. When someone asks a question the system embeds their query finds the closest matching chunks via cosine similarity and feeds that context into GPT-4o-mini to generate a response. The bot only talks about things Saketh has actually done. The backend is FastAPI with SQLite tracking knowledge entries, conversation logs, visitor leads, and unanswered questions. There is a password-protected admin panel for uploading new documents, adding specific Q&A pairs, viewing what people are asking, and seeing who left their email. OpenAI function calling handles the lead capture and logs questions the bot could not answer. Pushover notifications send real-time alerts when someone drops their email or asks something the bot does not know. Deployed on Hugging Face Spaces.

Project: Using AI to Explore What Landlords Care About in Promoting Housing Stability

Developed a large-scale NLP measurement pipeline to quantify landlord discourse across the U.S. rental market. Built a multi-stage LLM classification framework using GPT-4 and Llama with custom prompt schemas and structured output parsing to extract geographic signals and classify discussion topics from unstructured forum data. Achieved 98% precision in location extraction and 90% accuracy in topic classification, validated through human-in-the-loop review.

Applied unsupervised machine learning on topic embeddings to surface thematic groupings across five major categories including eviction processes, lease management, and conflict resolution. Conducted state-level comparative and temporal analysis revealing how landlord concerns shift in response to policy changes — for example, eviction discourse spiked during the 2021 moratorium and was disproportionately concentrated in states with complex regulatory frameworks like New York.

The research uncovered an underexplored finding: communication and conflict resolution emerged as a dominant theme, suggesting alignment between landlord and tenant goals that could inform preventive policy interventions. Findings were presented at the SSWR 2026 Annual Conference. This work demonstrates how AI-assisted qualitative analysis can surface actionable insights from large-scale unstructured data that traditional survey methods would miss.

Project: Automated Analysis of Local Government Housing and Eviction Policy Across the U.S.

Built an end-to-end data pipeline to construct the first nationwide structured dataset of local government housing policy content. Started with census-derived lists of every U.S. county, municipality, and borough. Used the Google Custom Search API to programmatically identify and validate official government websites for each jurisdiction, then built a Selenium-based automation framework to detect whether each site had a functional search engine.

For sites with search capability, injected policy-relevant keywords — housing policy, eviction process, rental assistance, tenant rights — captured returned results as MHTML for reproducible extraction, then parsed and extracted the underlying documents and text content. For the analysis layer, implemented a retrieval-augmented generation workflow using GPT-4 with semantic reranking to filter extracted content by relevance before passing it to the LLM for structured summarization and policy comparison across jurisdictions.

This created a nationally scoped, machine-readable dataset of how local governments communicate housing and eviction policy online — something that previously required manual review by research teams. The analysis phase is currently ongoing, examining differences in policy language, enforcement approaches, and transparency across states and municipalities.
